Welcome

Welcome to Grandma’s Quilts, where every piece is crafted with heart, history, and a whole lot of love. My name is Ruth Carpenter. I’m 85 years old, born and raised right here in Avery County, North Carolina. I spent years working at Baxter’s and caring for elderly folks who needed a little extra help—because that’s just what we do around here.

These days, even though my hands ache from carpal tunnel and my eyesight isn’t what it used to be, quilting is still my joy. I can’t see to thread a needle anymore, so my daughter Cindy and my grandson Ryan help me set up my old-fashioned Singer pedal machine, and I do the rest by hand—just like I always have.

Every quilt I make is filled with warmth, memory, and purpose. I do it not just because I love it, but because I believe handmade things carry stories—and it’s my way of giving something meaningful back to the world, especially in a time where every bit of support counts.

Thank you for supporting handmade, heart-made work. It means the world to me, and I hope each quilt brings comfort and love into your home the way they’ve always brought it into mine.
